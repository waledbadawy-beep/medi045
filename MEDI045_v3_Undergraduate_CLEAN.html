📚 About This Module — Crisis Simulation
This station uses a Standard Simulation Module (SSM) — a structured, reproducible clinical scenario used in high-fidelity simulation training. The case presented (Intraoperative Anaphylaxis during Routine Induction) is a worked example of the SSM format. You are expected to:

① Study the scenario before the session (pre-briefing)
② Perform or observe the simulation in the skill lab
③ Self-assess each performance step below
④ Complete the SBAR handover exercise
⑤ Reflect using the structured debrief questions

Other SSM scenarios you may encounter include: Cannot Intubate Cannot Oxygenate (CICO), Malignant Hyperthermia, Massive Haemorrhage, High Spinal Block, Cardiac Arrest in the OR.
📋 Learning Objectives
1. Recognise the clinical triad of intraoperative anaphylaxis (cardiovascular + respiratory + cutaneous)
2. Execute the first-line and secondary management algorithm including IM adrenaline
3. Demonstrate Crisis Resource Management (CRM): leadership, delegation, closed-loop communication
4. Differentiate anaphylaxis from other causes of intraoperative hypotension and bronchospasm
5. Perform a structured SBAR handover to the ICU team
6. Participate constructively in post-simulation debrief
📖 Scenario Brief – Intraoperative Anaphylaxis
Patient: Jane Doe · 42F · 70kg · MRN 789123
Procedure: Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy (Elective) · ASA II
Hx: HTN (Lisinopril 10mg) · Seasonal allergies · NKDA
Pre-op vitals: BP 138/84 · HR 72 · SpO₂ 98% RA · Mallampati II
Induction: Fentanyl 100mcg IV → Propofol 140mg IV → Rocuronium 50mg IV
T+2 min: HR↑110 · BP↓85/45 · Erythematous rash on chest/neck
T+5 min: BP↓70/40 · HR 125 · SpO₂↓92% · Peak PAP↑45cmH₂O · Urticaria
If no IM Adrenaline: BP↓55/30 → Bradycardia 45 → PEA arrest → CPR
Resolution: IM Adrenaline → BP↑110/60 · HR↓95 · PAP↓25 · SpO₂↑100%
🧠 Anaphylaxis Management Algorithm
🔴 IMMEDIATE (within 60 seconds of recognition):
1. STOP all potential triggers (IV infusions, latex exposure)
2. CALL FOR HELP — announce "This is anaphylaxis, I need help NOW"
3. ADRENALINE 1:1000 — 0.5mg (0.5ml) IM anterolateral thigh (preferred)
⚠️ IM Adrenaline is FIRST LINE — do not delay for IV access
🟡 SECONDARY (within 5 minutes):
4. Increase FiO₂ to 100% · Confirm airway is secured (intubate if not)
5. Aggressive IV fluid bolus: 1–2L crystalloid (LR or NS) rapidly
6. Bronchospasm: Salbutamol 2.5–5mg via nebuliser or ETT / IV MgSO₄
7. IV Hydrocortisone 200mg + IV Chlorphenamine 10mg (second-line)
8. If refractory: Adrenaline infusion (0.1–1 mcg/kg/min) or vasopressin
🟢 IF CARDIAC ARREST (PEA):
→ Start CPR immediately · Follow ACLS PEA algorithm
→ IV Adrenaline 1mg (1:10,000) every 3–5 minutes
→ Consider prolonged resuscitation (anaphylaxis mediators take time to clear)
